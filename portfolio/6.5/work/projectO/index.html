Project O

As a designer at Saggezza, I was embedded as an internal designer at a Fortune 500 client for over a year. While there, I implemented UX best practices into their internal and external applications, one of which was Project O. Confidential information and names has been changed/omitted.

Context

Project O is an existing internal application that automates the merging of several lists of data, and distributes the data of the lists to several machines on the production floor based on machine capacity and timeline.

Strategy

We began implementing a version of Lean UX which entailed regular, inclusive work sessions with key stakeholders, as well as regular involvement of end users for observations, clarification, and feedback. User Experience / Business Analyst / Development Lead / End Users

Research

It was important that we establish a good relationship with the end users, John and Jane.

Production

Producing the vessel began by creating a plaster mould from the final clay positive model, followed by casting, adjusting, drying, and glazing.

Results

Our team's efforts in improving UX and backend processes reduced user task time from 1-2 hours to ~15 minutes, freeing up the end users to work on other tasks and for the business to run more efficiently.
