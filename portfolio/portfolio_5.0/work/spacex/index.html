SpaceX Animation


As an astronomy and space exploration enthusiast, I wanted to exercise my CSS animation skills while paying homage to SpaceX for leading the advancement of humankind to the stars.

Concept

This animation is meant to display both SpaceX's present and future. Currently, SpaceX has acheived bringing their Falcon 9 Rocket and Dragon Capsule into orbit, rendevous with the International Space Station. It has successfully demonstrated technology allowing for terrestrial return landings on earth. By 2026, SpaceX plans to have humans on Mars.

Sketches / Brainstorming

Initial ideas were sketched out on what to include, how to present it, how it would be coded, etc. With a multitude of elements potentially involved in the animation, organization was key. Once all initial ideas were documented, they were sorted into heirarchies and grouped accordingly.

jQuery Prototype & Proof of Concept

To quickly guage feasibility, a rudimentary prototype was built and animated using jQuery. It was quickly discovered that the volume of animations was particularly heavy for browsers to process. I then decided to rebuild the animations using CSS.

Mockups

The animated visual graphics were created using Illustrator and Photoshop, based on concept sketches, visual references from watching SpaceX Falcon 9/Dragon launches, and the official images from the SpaceX website.

Development

One of the parameters I set for myself were that It should work in mobile view as well as desktop. This proved quite difficult, but with enough patience and knowledge, media queries and relative sizing/positioning proved to be most helpful.
