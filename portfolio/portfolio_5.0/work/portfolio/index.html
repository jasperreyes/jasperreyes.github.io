Portfolio Website


Designing and developing this portfolio site was a unique personal challenge, requiring the exercise of newfound web skills while learning even more along the way. In particular, one of the greatest challenges was keeping the site as simple and practical as possible, while imparting subtle elements of personality in the design.While the final result has diverged slightly from the diagrams as better solutions were often found during the coding stage, the basis of the site relied heavily on this work.

Information Hierarchy & Flow

The initial brainstorming and quick idea generation provided a starting point for the overall direction of the site's design and development.

Information Hierarchy & Flow

This diagram provides the foundation for both the site's content and information, as well as the flow of that information.

Main Page Wireframe

The scrolling layout provides a straightforward UI, presenting the information in a logical sequence. It works well on desktops and adapts well to mobile formats.

Project Page Wireframe

Upon reaching a project page, the navbar remains fixed and an additional button at the bottom of the screen allows the user to leave easily if/when desired.

Palette Explorations

In order to maximize attention to the portfolio content, a consistently neutral and subtle palette was chosen with the exception of green, which is used for branding, indicating clickability, and providing mouseover feedback.
